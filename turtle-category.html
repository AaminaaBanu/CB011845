Turtle category


Sea turtle


Green Turtle



Green turtle is one of the largest species of turtle commonly found in Sri Lanka. They possess a flattened body with a tear shaped shell, which is Blackish Grey in colour. Most adult turtle weigh in between 68kg and 190kgs, while some weigh at about 315kgs.

They are called as Green Turtle because of the green layers found under their shells. They eat only on marine vegetations as Algae and Sea grass. They are mainly found in Tropical waters of the ocean.



Olive Ridley



They are found in warm waters of Indian Ocean. It has Black heart shaped upper exoskeleton, which turns Olive Green during its adulthood. Its weight does not exceed 50kg. A large group of females turtles come to the ashore at once for nesting, where this phenomenon is called as “arribada”. Their main source of food is Jelly fish. They also have an average life span of 50 years.



Leatherback



one of the largest turtle living, and is the 4th most heaviest turtle in the modern day. It has a large Greyish-black teardrop shaped body covered with skin and oily flesh. It grows up to 2 feet long and it weighs more than 900 kgs. It mainly feeds on jelly fish and other small sea animals.



Hawksbill



They can be mostly found in coral reefs and shallow lagoons. This species is well known for their beautiful shell, as well they are listed under endangered animals due to hunters seeking its beautiful tortoise shell. It is dark golden brown in colour with reddish orange streaks. It has a thick hard upper shell, and its long head which looks like a hawk’s beak is the reason why it is called as hawksbill turtle.

Loggerhead



These turtles are mostly found in salty water deep in the ocean. They are also found in bays, lagoons and salty marshes during the female nesting periods. They are reddish brown in colour with a heart shaped top shell. The adult is about 3 feet in length and about 113kg in weight. They are carnivores and mainly eat on marine invertebrates. Loogerhead’s life span is only about 50 years approximately.
